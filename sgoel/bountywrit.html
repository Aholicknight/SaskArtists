Bounty

 

This is a work about pedophilia in ethnically isolated communities and how those communities choose to protect themselves/the abuser/the children.

The piece was exhibited in a small gallery space (approx. 12' X 12')

In the centre of the room was a low, square, white pedestal (2.5' X 2.5') on which there was a pile of 1000 child sized ears made of kool-aid scented/coloured play ded/coloured play dough.

In one corner of the room there was pedestal (3' tall, 1' X 1' square on top) which acted as an audio station. Extending out from a hole in the pedestal was a head set which played a digitally manipulated cycle of a lullaby sung in Hindi overlayed by the sound of an infant crying. Beside the headset was a 5 line statement :

he headset was a 5 line statement :

bounty hunter

pedophile

silence

children

sacrifice come, take of this bounty

 

The choice of the subject matter was the result of personal experience; the choice of imagery came from the images of bounty hunting in the Vietnam war - each side hunting the othernting the other, and the practise of collecting the ears of the hunted as proof of their deaths. In many ways I equate the bounty hunter with the pedophile; both are hunting, but the paedophile doesn’t take life but rather childhood.

 

facets of the work:

The language expresses double meanings: bounty as plenty; children as the bounty of society; bounty as the prize or catch.

The brightly coloured ears are both alluring and revolting. It is difficult to understand how one is supposed to react to them. They are ears, but can’t hear - in North American culture ears are also a symbol of speaking to some extent - it is the two sided street - without being able to heed street - without being able to hear are we able to speak? Are these the ears of the silenced community not speaking against internal against internal crimes, or of the children who can not be heard? Finally the headset amplifies the quality of isolation by forcing the viewer to place something over their own ears - thereby blocking out the room to some degree. As well, the sounds are confusing and threatening; the song is in another language, and it is difficult to tell if is supposed to sooth the child or terrify it.

The viewer is left with the feeling that the castrated ears are the only ones who could understand the sounds from the headset - but they are deaf and incapable.
