Data Base Implementation

First Normal Form: All fields/records/areas are atomic.

Second Normal Form: Implies that there exists a unique primary key and that all attributes are functionally dependant on that key.

Third Normal Form: Implies that second normal form criteria is met and that no transitive dependencies exist.

Examples:

  • Mother gives birth to child (has mother)

    Mother (MotherID, Name, Birth Date)
    Child (ChildID, Name, Birth Place, Birth Date, MotherID)

    Note: MotherID is the primary key of Mother()
    ChildID is the primary key of Child()
    MotherID is a foreign key found in Child()

    UNION unites Mother (belongs to UNION)
    UNION unites Father (belongs to UNION)

    Mother (MotherID, Name, Birth Date)
    Father (FatherID, Name, Birth Date)
    UNION (MotherID, FatherID) OR
    UNION (MotherID, FatherID, StartTime, EndTime)

Note: the second example allows for multiple instances of UNION.
